Core Idea

IP Finds The Host. Port Finds The App.

A socket combines an IP address, transport protocol, and port number, such as 192.168.1.10:443/tcp. If a service is listening, it is waiting for traffic on that port.

TCP

TCP

Connection-oriented, ordered, and reliable. Common for web, SSH, email, SMB, databases, and remote desktop.

UDP

UDP

Connectionless and lightweight. Common for DNS, DHCP, NTP, VoIP, VPNs, discovery, and streaming.

LST

Listening

A listening service accepts inbound traffic. Firewalls, access controls, and patching decide how risky that exposure is.

Port Number Ranges

The Three Big Buckets

0-1023 Well-known ports

Standard services like SSH, DNS, HTTP, HTTPS, SMTP, and NTP. On many systems, binding these ports requires elevated privileges.

1024-49151 Registered ports

Common vendor and application services, including databases, admin tools, logging, and many custom apps.

49152-65535 Dynamic or ephemeral ports

Usually temporary client-side ports used for outbound connections, though operating systems can vary their ephemeral range.
